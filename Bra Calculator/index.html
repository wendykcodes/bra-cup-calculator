POPULAR MYTHS

Myth 1: A bigger cup size means bigger breasts.

Not necessarily. Cup size is the volume of the breast relative to the bandsize, so someone with a larger band may have a larger cup size even if their breast volume is similar to someone with a smaller band size.

Myth 2: If your cup size is "DD", it means you have really large breasts.

A "DD" cup can refer to a wide range of breast sizes depending on the band size paired with.

Myth 3: Your bra size stays the same throughout your life.

Your bra size can change due to various factors (e.g, weight changes, pregnancy, age), so it is important to check your measurements regularly.

Myth 4: You can't wear a smaller cup size if you're larger in the band.

Someone with a larger band size can still wear a smalleer cup size. The key is to find the right fit, which involves both the band and the cup working together to provide comfort and support.

Myth 5: Bra cup sizes are the same across all brands.

Many brands use slightly different sizing systems, which is why trying on bras from different stores or brans may result in a different fit. Always prioritise comfort and fit over the number on the tag.
